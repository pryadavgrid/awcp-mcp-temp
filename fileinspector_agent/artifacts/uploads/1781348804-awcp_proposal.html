Client Proposal · Technical Specification

Agent Workforce Control Plane

A governance layer that takes control of the AI agents already running across your business — without rebuilding any of them.
For: VP of Operations For: Head of AI Governance Non-disruptive · Stack-agnostic

You don't have a problem building agents — you have a problem controlling the ones already working in the background. They run on different stacks, were built by different teams, and are now allowed to change real systems. AWCP is not another agent builder and not another dashboard. It is the control layer that sits between them.

1 The Vision & Core Value

What it is, in plain terms.
The 30-second pitch · "Air Traffic Control Tower"

Your agents are like a busy airport full of aircraft from different makers, each flying its own route. What's missing is the control tower. AWCP is that tower: it doesn't redesign the planes — it sits above them, sees every flight in one place, and holds the authority no single plane should hold itself: clearance to take a risky action. If conditions turn bad, it doesn't crash the fleet — it puts planes in a safe holding pattern and lands them cleanly.

One line: a single tower above a fragmented fleet — without grounding the fleet to rebuild it.

The 3 things an executive actually gets

Policy-gated actions

🛡 Smart Safe-Guards

Agents can read and recommend freely, but can't permanently change anything high-risk without passing a check. Stops a confident-but-wrong agent from making an irreversible mistake.

Progressive autonomy

🎚 The Dimmer Switch

When trouble appears it dims the agent — tighter limits, safer mode, finally "recommend-only" — instead of a hard crash. Work keeps running; risk drops.

Evidence ledger

↩ The Ultimate Undo

If a process pauses or fails, you resume from the exact checkpoint and keep a tamper-evident record of who did what, under which policy. Your recovery tool and your audit defense.

Most tools give you one of these. The control-plane model is designed to give you all three as one loop.

2 How It Takes Control (Step by Step)

How AWCP governs agents that are already running — without stopping the engines underneath.
1

Discovery & Ingestion

Runtime Adapters watch the existing stacks and pick up live workflow events as normalized signals. It only reads — the agents keep running exactly as they are.

First, put every plane on one screen.

2

The Registry Gate & Quarantine

Every agent is cataloged with its owner and runtime. Any agent missing safety hooks is put in read-only quarantine — visible and analyzable, but blocked from changing anything until it's properly instrumented.

No takeoff clearance until the transponder is confirmed.

3

Out-of-Band Evaluation (Separation of Powers)

Risky requests are judged by the Policy Engine in a separate environment from the agent. The agent cannot read, edit, or rewrite the rules that govern it — the thing being judged doesn't hold the gavel.

The pilot can't approve their own flight plan.

4

Durable Hold & Expiring Tokens

High-risk actions are placed on a durable pause (it can wait minutes or days without losing state) while a human issues a short-lived, signed approval token — good for one action, one branch, one time window.

A key cut for one door, for ten minutes — not a master key.

5

Automated Remediation

The Patch Generator automatically drafts the instrumentation code an under-instrumented agent needs, and routes it to the owning team for review — a real path out of quarantine, not just a flag.

Don't just say the part is missing — hand over the part, pre-fitted.

Discover→ Quarantine→ Evaluate out-of-band→ Hold + human token→ Remediate

4 The 90-Day Proof Slice

Low-risk and non-disruptive: connect to one workflow, prove the primitives, then scale.
Weeks 1–3
Observe

See, don't touch

Connect to one workflow, discover and register its agents, show the inventory and quarantine status. No writes gated yet — just prove we can see the fleet without disrupting it.

Weeks 4–7
Gate

Gate one action

Pick one high-value, state-changing action. Show the durable pause + expiring human token: it holds safely, a human approves a single-use token, the action proceeds, everything is recorded. Show a denial too.

Weeks 8–11
Degrade

Prove graceful degradation

Trigger a controlled failure and show the dimmer switch: drop to recommend-only instead of crashing, then resume from checkpoint. Hand the team an auto-generated instrumentation patch.

Week 12
Decide

Evidence & decision

Produce the replayable evidence ledger for every governed action — identity, policy result, token, context hash, resume pointer. The audit-grade artifact your risk and security stakeholders judge against agreed success criteria.

At day 90: one workflow under genuine control — proven to gate, hold with a human token, degrade gracefully, recover, and produce an audit-grade record — on your existing stack, with nothing rebuilt.

Why this is not "just another AI tool"

The builders (Bedrock, frameworks) make agents act; the monitors (Fiddler-class tools) tell you what they did. AWCP is the only layer whose job is to stand in the path of the risky action — on every runtime at once — and decide, with a human and a record, whether it proceeds. For a business whose agents can now change real systems, that layer isn't optional; it's the difference between automation you can defend and automation you have to hope about.
